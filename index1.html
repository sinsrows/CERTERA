Art of Ensaymada

we create delicious memories

Ensaymada is a delightful and indulgent pastry that hails from the Philippines, embodying a rich history and a fusion of cultural influences. This delicacy is a type of soft, sweet bread that is typically coiled into a spiral shape, generously topped with butter, sugar, and often grated cheese, creating a heavenly blend of flavors and textures.

The origins of ensaymada can be traced back to the Spanish colonial era, when Spanish bakers introduced their expertise in breadmaking to the Philippines. The name "ensaymada" itself is derived from the Spanish word "ensaimada," which refers to a Mallorcan pastry made with lard (saim in Catalan). Over time, as the recipe journeyed across oceans and cultures, Filipino bakers put their unique twist on this Spanish classic, resulting in the creation of the beloved ensaymada as it is known today.
